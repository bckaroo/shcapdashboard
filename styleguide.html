CE-01 ★
Integrate Clean Energy Systems into Public and Private Buildings
Expand renewable energy adoption, energy storage, and microgrids to enhance sustainability and resilience across Sleepy Hollow.
In Progress ★ Priority
42%
click to flip →

Full Details

Transitioning to on-site renewable energy systems will help Sleepy Hollow achieve its climate goals by reducing emissions and increasing energy independence. Public buildings will undergo feasibility assessments while private property owners receive technical support and incentives.

Sub-Actions

  • Conduct feasibility assessments for solar on municipal buildings
  • Install battery energy storage systems (BESS)
  • Develop microgrid pilot for critical facilities
  • Create incentive program for private solar adoption
Responsible:Village Board / DPW / Task Force
Funding:NY Green Bank, NY-Sun, NYSERDA Storage
Target:2027
CSC Points:25–40

CF-07
Implement Natural Shoreline and Living Shoreline Projects
Deploy nature-based solutions along the Hudson River waterfront to reduce coastal erosion, absorb storm surge, and restore habitat.
Not Started
0%
click to flip →

Full Details

Living shorelines use natural materials like marsh grasses, oyster reefs, and riprap to stabilize banks while providing ecosystem services. This approach reduces hard infrastructure costs and improves water quality.

Sub-Actions

  • Map priority shoreline segments for intervention
  • Partner with DEC on living shoreline permits
  • Install pilot marsh restoration at Horan's Landing
  • Monitor erosion and habitat recovery rates
Responsible:DPW / Environmental Advisory
Funding:NYSDEC, FEMA BRIC, NOAA
Target:2028

Annual Village Climate Action Progress Reports
Monitor, assess, and adapt the Climate Action Plan to ensure measurable progress toward sustainability goals.

Full Details

An annual reporting system evaluates progress on GHG reductions, energy efficiency, air quality, and sustainability metrics. Performance is benchmarked and strategies adjusted based on real-time data.

Sub-Actions

  • Establish baseline GHG inventory
  • Create annual reporting template
  • Publish first annual progress report
  • Present findings to Board of Trustees
